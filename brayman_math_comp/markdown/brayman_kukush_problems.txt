1. (1-year) Prove that for every $n \in \mathbb{N}$ there exists a unique $t(n)>0$ such that $(t(n)-1) \ln t(n)=n$. Calculate $\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty}\left(t(n) \frac{\ln n}{n}\right)$.

2. (1-year) Let $\left\{a_{n}, n \geq 1\right\} \subset \mathbb{R}$ be a bounded sequence. Define

$$
b_{n}=\frac{1}{n}\left(a_{1}+\ldots+a_{n}\right), n \geq 1 .
$$

Assume that the set $A$ of partial limits of $\left\{a_{n}, n \geq 1\right\}$ coincides with the set of partial limits of $\left\{b_{n}, n \geq 1\right\}$. Prove that $A$ is either a segment or a single point. Prove or disprove the following: if $A$ is either a segment or a single point then $A$ and $B$ coincide.
